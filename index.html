The Problem

Other contractors
run your job like
it's their first one.

Missed inspections. Subs showing up before the framing is done. Decisions that should have been made in week one getting made in week three — when it's too late and it costs you money.

That's not a contractor problem. That's a coordination problem. And it's fixable.

Problem 01
Trades showing up out of sequence because nobody mapped the job before work started.
Problem 02
Client decisions made mid-project. Drain locations. Equipment selections. Blocking. Things that can't move after drywall closes.
Problem 03
No documentation. No phase gates. No accountability. Just a text thread and a gut feeling that something's about to go wrong.
Problem 04
Scope creep with no change order. Work done without written authorization. Margin disappears and nobody knows where it went.
How We Work

Every job is
sequenced before
we touch it.

Before a tool hits the site, we've mapped the execution sequence, locked the trade schedule, and confirmed every client decision that can't wait until later. This isn't project management theory. It's the way professional jobs actually get run.

📋

Pre-Construction Planning

We build your Project Execution Plan before Day 1. Scope, sequence, crew assignments, and critical notes — locked in writing before anyone mobilizes.

Phase Gate: Nothing moves without a plan
02
🗓

Trade Sequencing

Framing before plumbing. MEP inspection before air seal. The sequence is intentional and field-tested. We don't compress the buffer and we don't improvise the order.

Phase Gate: Inspection cleared before proceeding
03
🔒

Client Decisions Locked Early

Water heater. Valve brand. Equipment dimensions. Every decision that affects rough-in gets locked before the trade that needs it ever shows up on site.

Phase Gate: Decisions locked before trades mobilize
04
📸

Documentation at Every Phase

Rough-ins photographed before close-in. Change orders in writing before execution. Punch list signed before final payment is collected. Every time.

Phase Gate: Photo documentation before cover

Built-In Buffer. Always.

We build contingency into every schedule. Inspection delays, material lead times, unforeseen conditions — none of it is a surprise because we planned for it.

Phase Gate: Buffer never compressed
